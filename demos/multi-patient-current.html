↓ THIS IS THE FRICTION ↓

If you switch to Pick mode, here's what the chips look like today. The parser sees every ## H2 / ### H3 and emits a chip — patient headers and per-section headings mixed together. Tapping "HPI" copies whichever HPI follows the first one (Tommy's), and the patient context gets visually flattened.

Section paste — Pick mode (current behavior)

19 chips. "HPI", "Exam", "Assessment", "Plan" each appear multiple times. No visual cue for which patient each section belongs to once it's a chip.

↓ PROPOSED FIX (Option 1 from the design discussion) ↓

Same chip primitive, just grouped by patient. Section parser becomes patient-aware: detects H2 patient headers, treats subsequent sections as belonging to that patient until the next H2.
