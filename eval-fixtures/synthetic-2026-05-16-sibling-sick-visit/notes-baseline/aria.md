**Chief Complaint**
"My throat hurts."

**HPI**
Aria is a child presenting with sore throat and fever since Friday afternoon. Mother reports the sore throat is significant enough to cause pain with swallowing. She also complains of right ear pain. A fever of 101.4°F was noted Friday night, which responded to Tylenol; she has been afebrile since. Appetite is decreased, but she is drinking well. Associated symptoms include mild rhinorrhea. Mother denies any significant cough. Two siblings are also ill with similar symptoms.

**ROS**
- Constitutional: Subjective fever reported, resolved. No report of lethargy.
- HEENT: Endorses sore throat, odynophagia, right otalgia, and mild rhinorrhea. Denies significant cough.
- GI: Decreased appetite. No vomiting or diarrhea. Good oral intake of fluids reported.

**Exam**
- General: Alert and in no acute distress.
- HEENT:
    - Oropharynx: **Tonsils are erythematous and enlarged with exudate noted on the right.**
    - Otoscopic: **Right tympanic membrane is erythematous without bulge.** Left tympanic membrane is normal.
- Neck: **Tender, swollen anterior cervical lymphadenopathy noted.**
- Lungs: Clear to auscultation bilaterally.

**Assessment**
1. Pharyngitis, suspicious for streptococcal infection given fever, tonsillar exudate, tender anterior cervical adenopathy, and absence of cough.
2. Right Otalgia, likely referred from pharyngitis.

**Plan**
1. Pharyngitis:
    - Rapid strep antigen test performed in clinic today.
    - If positive: Will send a prescription for amoxicillin to the pharmacy.
    - If negative: Plan is for supportive care. No antibiotic will be prescribed at this time.
    - Continue Tylenol as needed for throat pain.
2. General:
    - Return precautions discussed, including worsening pain, difficulty breathing, or signs of dehydration.
    - Mother verbalizes understanding of the plan.

**Follow-up**
Will contact mother via text this afternoon with the rapid strep test result.
