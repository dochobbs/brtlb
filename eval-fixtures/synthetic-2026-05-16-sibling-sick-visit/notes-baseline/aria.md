**Chief Complaint**
"My throat hurts."

**HPI**
Aria is a school-aged female presenting with sore throat and fever starting Friday afternoon. The sore throat is described as painful to swallow. She also complains of right ear pain. Per mother, she had a fever to 101.4°F on Friday night, which responded to Tylenol. She has been afebrile since then. Appetite is decreased, but she is drinking well. Associated with mild rhinorrhea but no significant cough. Sick contacts at home (two siblings with similar symptoms).

**ROS**
- Constitutional: Subjective fever, resolved. Reports decreased appetite. No change in activity level noted.
- ENT: Endorses sore throat, odynophagia, right otalgia, and mild rhinorrhea. Denies cough.
- GI: No vomiting or diarrhea reported. Good PO intake of fluids.

**Exam**
- Oropharynx: **Tonsils are erythematous and enlarged with exudate noted on the right tonsil.**
- Otoscopic: **Right tympanic membrane is erythematous without bulge.** Left tympanic membrane is normal.
- Neck: **Tender anterior cervical lymphadenopathy.**
- Lungs: Clear to auscultation bilaterally.

**Assessment**
1. Pharyngitis, suspicious for streptococcal infection
2. Otalgia, right

**Plan**
1. **Pharyngitis:**
    - A rapid strep antigen test will be performed in the office today.
    - If the rapid strep test is positive, a prescription for amoxicillin will be sent to the pharmacy.
    - If the rapid strep test is negative, the plan is for supportive care. No antibiotic will be prescribed at this time.
    - Return to clinic if symptoms worsen, if she is not improving in 48 hours, or for any signs of dehydration (decreased urination, dry mouth, no tears).
2. **Otalgia:**
    - Likely referred pain from pharyngitis.
    - May use Tylenol or ibuprofen as needed for pain.

**Follow-up**
- Parent will be notified of the rapid strep test result via text message this afternoon.
