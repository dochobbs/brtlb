**Chief Complaint**
"I have a cough."

**HPI**
Theo presents with a cough that began on Saturday. Per mother, the cough was initially dry but is now "wet sounding" and productive of yellow sputum. Mother denies any fever or wheezing heard at home. She notes he has been "a little winded going up the stairs" but denies any chest tightness or significant trouble breathing.

Patient has a history of mild asthma which typically only flares with viral illnesses. He has not required albuterol since last fall.

**ROS**
- Constitutional: No fever.
- Respiratory: Endorses productive cough. Reports some dyspnea on exertion. Denies wheezing or chest tightness.

**Exam**
- Lungs: **Coarse breath sounds on the left with rhonchi.** No wheezing noted. Good air movement throughout.

**Assessment**
1. Asthma exacerbation, mild, triggered by URI
2. Viral Upper Respiratory Infection

**Plan**
1.  **Asthma Exacerbation:**
    - Will restart Albuterol MDI.
    - Plan: 2 puffs via spacer Q4 hours while awake for the next 3 days, then as needed for cough or wheezing.
    - Counseled patient and mother on the importance of using the spacer to ensure medication is delivered to the lungs.
    - Return precautions: Call or return for any increased work of breathing, wheezing, or need for albuterol more frequently than every 4 hours. Patient does not currently meet the threshold for oral steroids but will monitor closely.

**Follow-up**
