**Chief Complaint**
"I have a cough."

**HPI**
Theo presents with a cough that began on Saturday. Per mother, the cough was initially dry but is now "wet sounding" and productive of yellow sputum. There has been no fever. He has a history of mild asthma that flares with colds and has not required albuterol since last fall. Mother denies hearing any wheezing at home but notes he has been "a little winded going up the stairs." She denies any significant trouble breathing or chest tightness.

**ROS**
- Constitutional: No fever reported.
- Respiratory: Endorses productive cough and mild dyspnea on exertion. Denies wheezing or chest tightness.

**Exam**
- Lungs: **Coarse breath sounds with rhonchi on the left.** Good air movement throughout. No wheezing.

**Assessment**
1. Asthma exacerbation, mild, triggered by viral illness
2. Viral Upper Respiratory Infection

**Plan**
1.  **Asthma Exacerbation:**
    - Start albuterol MDI, 2 puffs via spacer every 4 hours while awake for the next 3 days, then as needed (PRN) for cough or wheezing.
    - A prescription for albuterol MDI and a spacer will be sent to the pharmacy.
    - Counseled patient and mother on the importance of using the spacer for effective medication delivery to the lungs.
    - Patient does not currently meet the threshold for oral steroids; will continue to monitor.
    - Return precautions: Call or return immediately for any increased work of breathing, new wheezing, or if requiring albuterol more frequently than every 4 hours.
