**Chief Complaint**
"pulling at her right ear"

**HPI**
Lucy is an 18-month-old female presenting with acute onset of right ear pulling and rhinorrhea, which began this morning upon waking. Mother reports she was cranky but had no fever at home. Appetite is slightly decreased for solids (refused oatmeal) but she is nursing well.

**ROS**
- Constitutional: No fever reported. Endorses crankiness.
- Ears: Reports pulling at right ear.
- Nose: Rhinorrhea.
- Respiratory: No work of breathing observed.
- GI: Tolerating breastmilk but refused solids this morning.

**Exam**
- General: Alert, in no acute distress.
- Ears:
    - Right: **Tympanic membrane is erythematous, dull, and slightly bulging with loss of landmarks.**
    - Left: Tympanic membrane is normal in appearance.
- Nose: Clear rhinorrhea present.
- Throat: Mild postnasal drip noted. Pharynx is non-erythematous.
- Lungs: Clear to auscultation bilaterally.

**Assessment**
1.  Right Acute Otitis Media
2.  Viral Upper Respiratory Infection
3.  Immunizations Due (deferred)

**Plan**
1.  **Right AOM:** Per AAFP guidelines for watchful waiting, will provide a safety-net prescription for amoxicillin.
    - Mother instructed to fill and start amoxicillin only if Lucy is not improving in 48 hours, or if symptoms worsen (e.g., development of high fever). If she is improving, the prescription should not be filled.
    - For analgesia, recommend ibuprofen 6 mL of children's suspension (100mg/5mL) every 6 hours as needed for ear pain. Do not exceed 4 doses in 24 hours.
2.  **Viral URI:** Supportive care. Daycare is acceptable as tolerated; ear infections are not contagious.
3.  **Immunizations:** Patient is due for Hepatitis A #1 and Varicella #2. These are deferred today due to acute illness. Plan to catch up at next visit.
4.  **Return Precautions:** Return to clinic sooner if she develops a high fever, is not improving in 48 hours, or if any other concerns arise.

**Follow-up**
- Return to clinic in 3-4 weeks for deferred immunizations.
