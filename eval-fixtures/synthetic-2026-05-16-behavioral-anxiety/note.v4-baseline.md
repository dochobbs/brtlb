**Subjective**

**Reason for Visit:** Follow-up for anxiety and school refusal.

**Course of Symptoms:**
Mother reports that patient's school refusal has significantly improved since the last visit. With implementation of a structured morning routine, a "worry-box" the night before, and curb-side drop-off, patient has attended school every day for the past three weeks. Mother notes, "She still cries sometimes in the car but she goes."

However, a new symptom of night waking began approximately three weeks ago. Mother reports this occurs about four nights per week. Patient wakes between 2:00 and 4:00 AM feeling "panicked" and is unable to fall back asleep, sometimes for up to two hours. She goes into her parents' bed and is able to fall asleep within ten minutes with her mother.

When asked about the content of her worries upon waking, patient states, "Sometimes it's school. Sometimes it's that someone is gonna die. Sometimes I don't know, I just feel scared." She specifies that she worries about her grandfather ("Pop") or her parents dying.

Mother also reports new complaints of stomach aches in the morning, but only on school days. No associated vomiting, weight loss, or changes in bowel habits.

**Stressors and Supports:**
A significant recent stressor was identified: patient's maternal grandfather had a heart attack three weeks ago. Mother notes the onset of night waking coincides with this event. The grandfather is reportedly "OK now, he's home," but the event was very frightening for the family. Patient is described as being very close to him.

Patient is currently in weekly therapy with her therapist, Allison.

**Safety / Risk:**
- **Suicidal/Self-Harm Ideation: Denied.** No discussion of suicidal ideation, self-harm, or harm to others.
- **Primary fear is of family members dying, specifically her grandfather, mother, and father.**

---

**Mental Status / Observation**

Patient is a well-appearing child, sitting quietly next to her mother. She was cooperative throughout the visit, making appropriate eye contact when addressed. She answered questions directly but with brief, one- or two-word responses. Speech was soft and clear. Mood was described by patient as "scared" at night. Affect was constricted but appropriate to the content of the discussion. Thought content was notable for anxious preoccupations regarding the health and safety of family members.

---

**Physical Examination**

**Vitals:** Unremarkable.
**General:** Well-appearing, in no acute distress.
**Cardiovascular:** Regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs.
**Respiratory:** Lungs clear to auscultation bilaterally.
**Abdomen:** Soft, non-tender, non-distended.

---

**Assessment**

1.  **Anxiety Disorder:** Patient's symptoms are consistent with her underlying anxiety, which has now manifested with a new theme of nocturnal panic and thanatophobia (fear of death/dying) following a significant family stressor (grandfather's heart attack). The morning school refusal has improved with behavioral interventions, while the new nighttime symptoms are currently unaddressed. Somatic complaints (stomach aches) are consistent with anxiety.
2.  **No acute safety risk identified.**

---

**Plan**

1.  **Medication:** Discussed the role of medication (SSRI, specifically sertraline) with mother. Given patient's age, the clear situational trigger for her new symptoms, and her positive response to behavioral interventions for school refusal, we will defer starting medication at this time. Plan to re-evaluate in 6-8 weeks; if no improvement in nighttime symptoms, we will reconsider pharmacotherapy. Mother is in agreement with this plan and expressed a preference to focus on behavioral strategies first.

2.  **Therapy:**
    *   Continue weekly therapy.
    *   Advised mother to update the therapist on the new onset night-waking and specific fears about family members dying.
    *   A brief clinical update will be sent to the therapist.

3.  **Behavioral Interventions:**
    *   **Night Waking:**
        *   Introduced the "worry-thank-you" cognitive technique for patient to use when she wakes up with anxious thoughts (e.g., "Thanks brain, I see you trying to keep people safe. They're safe. I'm going back to sleep.").
        *   Counseled mother on a gradual plan to help patient return to her own bed. When patient comes to parents' room, allow brief cuddling (~5 minutes), then walk her back to her room. Parent to stay with a reassuring touch (e.g., hand on back) for a couple of minutes before leaving. The goal is to slowly taper parental presence to re-establish her own room as a safe space.
    *   **Anxiety Regarding Grandfather:**
        *   Recommended increasing brief, low-pressure, positive contact with her grandfather to provide repeated, tangible evidence of his well-being.
        *   Family will implement nightly FaceTime calls with him before bed.
    *   **Somatic Symptoms:**
        *   Advised mother to validate the feeling ("I know your tummy hurts, that means you're feeling worried") while maintaining the expectation of going to school and reassuring patient that the feeling will improve upon arrival.

4.  **Follow-up:**
    *   Return to clinic in 6 weeks for follow-up.
    *   Instructed to contact us sooner if symptoms worsen or if new concerns arise.
