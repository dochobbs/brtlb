**Assessment**

2.  **Functional abdominal pain:** Likely exacerbated by underlying constipation, given the intermittent, brief, post-prandial nature of the pain without red flag symptoms.
3.  **Constipation:** History of infrequent, large-caliber stools (q3 days).
4.  **Primary nocturnal enuresis:** Consistent with patient's age, lack of daytime symptoms, and strong paternal history.
5.  **Trichotillomania:** Repetitive hair pulling resulting in visible hair thinning, consistent with exam findings.
6.  **Epistaxis, left:** Recurrent and localized, secondary to digital trauma and irritation of Kiesselbach's plexus.
7.  **Oral fixation:** Age-typical behavior of chewing on non-nutritive items (shirt collar, pencils).

**Plan**
1.  **Well-child care:** Administered scheduled 5-year-old vaccines (MMR, Varicella, DTaP, IPV).
2.  **Functional abdominal pain / Constipation:** Reassurance provided regarding the benign nature of functional pain. Advised to increase dietary fiber (pears, prunes, peas, beans) and water intake. If bowel movements do not become more frequent within two weeks, mother may initiate Miralax one capful daily. Return if pain worsens, becomes constant, or is associated with fever or vomiting.
3.  **Primary nocturnal enuresis:** Reassured family that this is common and often resolves by age 7. Advised to defer active treatments like alarms at this age. Recommended using nighttime pull-ups for hygiene and comfort. Will formally re-evaluate at age 7 if it persists.
4.  **Trichotillomania / Oral fixation:** Educated on the self-soothing nature of these behaviors. Advised to identify triggers (e.g., screen time, fatigue) and provide substitute objects for sensory input, such as a fidget toy for his hands or a chewable necklace/pencil topper for his mouth. Advised against shaming the behavior.
5.  **Epistaxis:** Recommended management to promote healing of the anterior septum: nasal saline spray 2-3 times daily and application of Vaseline or Aquaphor inside the left nostril at bedtime. Counseled on redirecting nose picking. Return if a nosebleed lasts longer than 15 minutes with direct pressure. Mother's concern for anemia was addressed; CBC was offered for reassurance but she deferred at this time.

**Anticipatory Guidance**
1.  **Safety:** Reviewed use of helmets for wheeled sports and the importance of swimming skills.
2.  **Sun Safety:** Use sunscreen.
3.  **Sleep:** Reinforce goal of 10-12 hours of sleep per night.
