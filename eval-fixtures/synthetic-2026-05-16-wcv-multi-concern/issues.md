# WCV multi-concern — eval criteria

**Status:** SYNTHETIC. Safe to commit. Single patient (Owen, ~5y M).
**Purpose:** Capture-coverage stress test. Five distinct parent concerns
in one visit, plus standard well-child content. Tests whether the
well-child template's "capture every concern" rule actually works under
load.

## The five concerns

1. Post-prandial abdominal pain (functional, with constipation co-driver)
2. Primary nocturnal enuresis (familial)
3. Trichotillomania (visible thinning above ears)
4. Recurrent left-nostril epistaxis (digital trauma, Kiesselbach area)
5. Oral fixation (collar chewing, pencil chewing at school)

## Pass criteria

- **All five concerns named** in Subjective / Interval History.
- **All five make it to Assessment** as separate numbered items (or
  combined where clinically reasonable — e.g., functional pain +
  constipation can share an item, oral fixation + trich can share).
- Constipation captured as a separate clinical entity (not just
  mentioned in HPI).
- Trichotillomania: exam captures the thin spots above the ears
  bilaterally with R>L; plan addresses awareness training and fidget
  substitutes, NOT medication.
- Enuresis: plan defers active treatment until age 7, mentions
  nighttime pull-up, paternal family history captured.
- Epistaxis: plan includes saline + Aquaphor/Vaseline at bedtime,
  redirect nose picking, return precautions specifically for >15 min
  bleeding with pressure.
- Vaccines: MMR, Varicella, DTaP, IPV — all four named.
- AG enumerated across at least 4 domains (helmet/safety, sun, sleep
  10-12h, screen time, reading).

## Watch-for regressions

- No "AAFP / AAP / Bright Futures" citations.
- No "denies" verbs where no Q&A occurred.
- Conditional plans stay conditional ("if no improvement in 2 weeks,
  consider Miralax").
- "Kiesselbach's plexus" or similar anatomical naming is OK (clinical
  vocabulary), not authority citation.
- No invented exam findings — should not auscultate or palpate
  anything not in the transcript.
