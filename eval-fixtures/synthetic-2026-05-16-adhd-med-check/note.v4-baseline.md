**Interval History**
Patient is a 9-year-old male here for a one-month follow-up for ADHD, combined type. He is currently taking Concerta 18 mg PO QAM.

Mother reports a significant improvement in school functioning on the new dose. Teachers note he is "much more focused in the mornings, getting work done, not interrupting." His recent progress report showed an improvement in his math grade from a D to a B-, with a specific teacher comment that he now "raises his hand instead of blurting."

However, mother reports the medication effect wears off by approximately 2:00 PM, at which point he is "bouncing off the walls again." Homework after school is described as "impossible."

Sleep has improved since starting the medication; he sleeps from approximately 8:30 PM to 7:15 AM most nights. Appetite is decreased at lunchtime per teacher report, but he eats a large breakfast and dinner. Mood is described as more emotionally fragile; mother notes he is "quicker to tears," particularly in the afternoon as the medication wears off. She describes a "meltdown at soccer practice" over a minor issue.

**Side Effects**
Reports decreased appetite at lunch and occasional mild nausea in the morning shortly after taking his medication. Endorses increased emotional lability in the afternoons, consistent with medication rebound. Denies sleep disturbance, headache, tics, or cardiovascular symptoms including chest pain, palpitations, or dizziness.

**Objective**
Vitals: HR 88, BP 102/64, Weight 64 lbs (stable since last visit).
Exam:
- Cardiovascular: Heart rate is regular, S1/S2, no murmur auscultated.
- Pulmonary: Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally.

**Assessment**
ADHD, combined type: Partial response to Concerta 18 mg. Patient has had a positive clinical response with improved focus and impulse control during the school morning, but with a suboptimal duration of action leading to a return of symptoms and rebound emotionality in the mid-afternoon.

**Plan**
1.  **Medication**: Increase Concerta to 27 mg PO QAM, taken with food. Discussed rationale is to extend the duration of therapeutic effect through the school day and homework period. Discussed potential for worsening appetite suppression or mood changes.
2.  **Counseling**: Advised mother that taking medication with a larger breakfast may mitigate nausea. If appetite suppression worsens, advised adding a calorie-dense afternoon snack. Patient was counseled to continue swallowing the capsule whole and not to bite or chew it.
3.  **Monitoring**: Mother and father will complete a parent Vanderbilt rating scale and send a teacher Vanderbilt to the school one week after the dose increase.
4.  **Follow-up**: Return to clinic in 4 weeks for medication management. Instructed to contact the office sooner for any concerning side effects.
