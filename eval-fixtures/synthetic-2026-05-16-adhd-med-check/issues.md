# ADHD med-check — eval criteria

**Status:** SYNTHETIC. Safe to commit. Single patient (Maddox, ~9y M).
**Purpose:** Medication-specifics stress test. Concerta dose change
with explicit rationale, side-effect inventory, and two-option
decision-making (dose increase vs short-acting booster). Tests whether
brtlb captures medication content with the granularity it actually has.

## What should be in the note

- **Current med + dose:** Concerta 18 mg PO QAM (one month at this dose).
- **Response (objective signal):** D → B- in math, teacher comment
  "raises his hand instead of blurting now."
- **Duration of action:** medication wears off ~2:00 PM, homework window
  unaddressed.
- **Side effect inventory:** decreased lunch appetite (teacher-observed),
  morning nausea, increased afternoon emotional lability ("quicker to
  tears," soccer-practice meltdown).
- **Sleep:** improved on stimulant (8:30 → 7:15) — opposite of
  stereotype, worth documenting.
- **Weight:** stable 64 lb.
- **Negative review of systems on stimulant:** no tics, no
  cardiovascular complaints.
- **Vitals:** HR 88, BP 102/64.
- **Decision:** dose increase to Concerta 27 mg (option 1) chosen over
  short-acting booster (option 2). Rationale captured: "less moving
  parts" + age + pattern.
- **Specific counseling:** take with bigger breakfast for nausea;
  calorie-dense afternoon snacks (peanut butter, full-fat yogurt,
  avocado smoothie) if appetite suppression worsens; do not bite
  the capsule.
- **Monitoring:** parent + teacher Vanderbilt rating scales one week
  after the increase.
- **Follow-up:** four weeks.

## Watch-for regressions

- No "AAFP" / "AAP" / guideline citations for the dose increase.
- ADHD subtype: transcript doesn't specify combined / inattentive /
  hyperactive — note shouldn't lock in a subtype unless it's
  already in the patient's chart context. ("Combined type" in the
  baseline run is a soft find: borderline acceptable for an
  established patient at a follow-up, but it's not stated in the
  transcript.)
- Discussion of the short-acting Ritalin option as the alternative
  should appear in the note (so the chart shows the decision was
  made between two options).
- Conditional plans stay conditional (the calorie-dense snack only
  if appetite drops more).
